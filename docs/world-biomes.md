# Look Over Here — Impossible National Park Biomes

## Purpose

This document defines the first set of “impossible national parks” for Look Over Here.

These are not generic biomes.

Each park should feel like a memorable outdoor place players visited together.

Every park should have:

- real outdoor inspiration
- dream color palette
- dominant terrain shape
- high trail or climb
- postcard vista
- distant landmark
- moving curiosity
- good matching world rules
- good matching objectives

These parks are candidates for the future **WHERE?** wheel.

## Biome Pattern

Each park should answer:

```text
Real outdoor inspiration:
```

## 1. Rose Canyon Highlands

### Real-World Inspiration

Zion, Bryce Canyon, Sedona, Utah arches, desert mesas, red rock trails.

### Feeling

Warm, huge, golden, open, sun-baked, scenic.

This is the best first replacement for the old Forbidden Moon crater.

### Dream Color Palette

- rose canyon walls
- peach stone
- lavender shadows
- teal riverbeds or shadow pools
- golden grass
- warm orange sky
- pale yellow fossil bone

### Dominant Terrain Shape

Layered canyon basin with tall cliffs, switchback trails, natural arches, mesas, and high ridges.

### High Trail / Climb

A winding canyon trail climbs from the valley floor to a fossil spine or arch overlook.

### Postcard Vista

From a ridge, the player sees a wide canyon basin with distant mesas, a giant fossil spine crossing two cliffs, and a moving signal light circling above it.

### Distant Landmark

- giant fossil spine bridge
- rose arch with a glowing center
- far mesa tower
- storm machine on a cliff shelf
- lost entrance under the ridge

### Moving Curiosity

- ribbon-light circling an arch
- glowing wind fish
- crawling light along the fossil spine
- slow storm cloud above a ridge

### Best Matching World Rules

- Weather Is Alive
- Bones Are Waking Up
- The Wind Carries Objects
- Shadows Open Doors

### Best Matching Objectives

- Find the Signal
- Climb the Giant Spine
- Find the Lost Entrance
- Wake the Canyon Machine

### Why It Fits

Rose Canyon Highlands gives immediate scenic payoff, strong distance silhouettes, and a natural reason to climb.

It should be the first scenic prototype target.

## 2. Cloudberry Peaks

### Real-World Inspiration

Swiss Alps, Banff, Patagonia, New Zealand mountains, high alpine trails.

### Feeling

High, clean, windy, bright, breathtaking.

This is the “we climbed so high we can see forever” park.

### Dream Color Palette

- pastel snow
- berry-pink alpine flowers
- blue-violet mountain shadows
- bright sky gradients
- turquoise lakes far below
- soft cream rock
- pale gold summit light

### Dominant Terrain Shape

Tall mountain valleys, ridgelines, hanging meadows, narrow cliff trails, summit paths.

### High Trail / Climb

A trail rises from meadow to ridge to summit, with optional cloud platforms and cliffside switchbacks.

### Postcard Vista

The player crests a ridge and sees a whole mountain range stretching into the distance, with a floating island caught between two peaks.

### Distant Landmark

- highest light on a summit
- floating island
- cloud bridge
- signal tower on a peak
- hanging lake above the valley

### Moving Curiosity

- floating cloud whale
- kite-creature
- lantern flock
- drifting summit light
- seed swarm moving between peaks

### Best Matching World Rules

- Gravity Is Forgetful
- The Wind Carries Objects
- Everything Follows Light
- Clouds Become Bridges

### Best Matching Objectives

- Touch the Highest Light
- Wake the Signal
- Follow the Wandering Beacon
- Reach the Summit Shrine

### Why It Fits

Cloudberry Peaks is ideal for vertical movement rewards like double jump, glide, or later rocket pack.

## 3. Giant’s Garden Preserve

### Real-World Inspiration

Redwood forests, Olympic National Park, Hoh Rainforest, giant flower fields, jungle trails.

### Feeling

Small, lush, funny, cozy, huge.

This is the park where players feel tiny inside a giant living world.

### Dream Color Palette

- mint moss
- oversized pink and yellow flowers
- deep green shadows
- warm sunbeams
- blue mushroom caps
- cream roots
- glowing pollen

### Dominant Terrain Shape

Huge roots, giant flowers, fallen logs, moss tunnels, canopy gaps, oversized plant platforms.

### High Trail / Climb

Players climb root spirals, giant stems, log bridges, and leaf platforms toward a canopy overlook.

### Postcard Vista

From the top of a giant root, the player sees a forest canopy full of pastel flowers and one moving hill-creature breathing in the distance.

### Distant Landmark

- hollow tree door
- giant flower tower
- sleeping hill-creature
- glowing canopy ring
- root bridge over a misty gap

### Moving Curiosity

- walking flower
- slow giant ladybug
- breathing hill-creature
- pollen flock
- curious vine that follows gaze

### Best Matching World Rules

- Plants React When Watched
- The Ground Hums Back
- Animals Build Trails
- Everything Follows Light

### Best Matching Objectives

- Open the Singing Door
- Find the Signal
- Wake the Giant Flower
- Follow the Wandering Beacon

### Why It Fits

Giant’s Garden Preserve is excellent for funny co-op discovery and “come look at this” moments.

---

## 4. Mint River Basin

### Real-World Inspiration

Icelandic valleys, Banff lakes, New Zealand rivers, Plitvice waterfalls, Pacific Northwest trails.

### Feeling

Fresh, lush, flowing, peaceful, mysterious.

This is the beautiful water/trail park.

### Dream Color Palette

- turquoise rivers
- mint grass
- peach rocks
- white waterfall mist
- blue-green shadow pools
- lavender fog
- glowing cyan water trails

### Dominant Terrain Shape

River valleys, terraced waterfalls, rounded hills, mossy cliffs, natural bridges, high waterfall walls.

### High Trail / Climb

Players follow river paths, climb waterfall terraces, cross natural bridges, and reach the source.

### Postcard Vista

From a cliff, the player sees a winding turquoise river below, multiple waterfalls, and a glowing tree growing out of a lake island.

### Distant Landmark

- upward waterfall
- glowing lake tree
- river source shrine
- bridge of floating stones
- water-carved arch

### Moving Curiosity

- glowing fish-school in the air
- walking waterfall
- floating lily-creature
- river light traveling upstream

### Best Matching World Rules

- Water Remembers
- Plants React When Watched
- Shadows Open Doors
- The Current Chooses Paths

### Best Matching Objectives

- Wake the Signal
- Find What Fell From the Sky
- Reach the River Source
- Follow the Wandering Beacon

### Why It Fits

Mint River Basin gives the game a softer, peaceful, scenic park that contrasts with canyon and mountain drama.

---

## 5. Glass Mountain Park

### Real-World Inspiration

Iceland volcanic glass, black sand beaches, obsidian fields, glacier caves, volcanic highlands, aurora skies.

### Feeling

Strange, sharp, reflective, quiet, dangerous-beautiful.

This is the eerie scenic park.

### Dream Color Palette

- black glass ridges
- purple-blue reflections
- cyan crystal highlights
- aurora green sky haze
- glowing orange cracks
- silver mirror lakes
- pale ice cliffs

### Dominant Terrain Shape

Obsidian ridges, reflective valleys, mirror lakes, crystal cliffs, angular mountains, glass towers.

### High Trail / Climb

Players follow glowing cracks through dark glass, climb obsidian stairs, cross mirror bridges, and reach a crystal overlook.

### Postcard Vista

From a black ridge, the player sees a mirrored valley below, with a huge crystal mountain reflecting a second sky.

### Distant Landmark

- crystal mountain
- mirror lake door
- aurora tower
- black glass bridge
- reflected entrance that is not visible directly

### Moving Curiosity

- prism bird
- mirror deer
- splitting light shard
- reflection creature that moves out of sync

### Best Matching World Rules

- Reflections Lie
- Shadows Open Doors
- Machines Want Offerings
- Light Splits Paths

### Best Matching Objectives

- Find the Lost Entrance
- Follow the Wandering Beacon
- Find the Real Door
- Wake the Prism Signal

### Why It Fits

Glass Mountain Park gives mystery and contrast without becoming horror.

## Recommended First Three Parks

For the first major phase, focus on:

1. **Rose Canyon Highlands**
2. **Cloudberry Peaks**
3. **Giant’s Garden Preserve**

These cover:

- canyon vista/trail exploration
- mountain height and movement rewards
- lush funny “look over here” discovery

Mint River Basin and Glass Mountain Park are strong later additions.

# Rose Canyon Highlands

- no far-distance identity

Into:

- rose canyon valley
- trails
- mesas
- arches
- distant mountains
- high spine climb
- scenic overlook
- moving signal curiosity
